The cast

Each agent carries intelligence, not raw data. That's the portability we're arguing for: compact signals apps already derived about you, made available to your agent — at the right sensitivity.

The paradigm, in plain sight

01

Progressive discovery

Ten MCP servers × a hundred tools is a thousand poisoned prompts. Agents should search compact representations first, then pull full content only when a match is warranted.

02

Intelligence portability

Gmail already knows who you reply to within a day. Amazon already knows your size. The portable asset is not the raw archive — it's the derived signal, exposable to your agent on demand.

03

Disclosure cards

Borrowed from Alvin Roth–style market design: the information to reveal is itself the good being traded. Each side offers a shape before a payload; commits only when shapes match.

04

Ephemerality

The enclave is a room with a timer. When the task concludes, inter-agent reasoning is wiped; only the outcome crosses the threshold. Two-sided markets need two-sided forgetting.

05

Alpha, not beta

Aggregators (beta) are table stakes. The real value lives in friend-of-friend alpha — the sushi chef's recommendation, the analyst's paper invoice. Me-agents surface it without flattening it.

06

Minimum viable protocol

Be too prescriptive and nobody ships. The bet: fix the agent-to-agent envelope and the missing MCP analysis tools. Let everyone bring their own context-gathering to the enclave.
